# SOP-014: Delayed Shipment Escalation Process

**Document Owner:** Logistics Operations
**Version:** 3.2
**Last Reviewed:** 2025-11-01

## Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure defines how staff escalate shipments that
miss their committed delivery date.

## Definitions
- **Delayed shipment:** Any shipment that has not been delivered within 24
  hours of its committed delivery date.
- **Critical shipment:** A delayed shipment for a Tier-1 customer or with a
  value exceeding $50,000.

## Escalation Process
1. **Tier 1 (0–24 hours late):** The assigned logistics coordinator contacts
   the carrier directly and logs the delay in the Shipment Tracker. No
   management involvement required.
2. **Tier 2 (24–48 hours late):** The coordinator escalates to the Regional
   Logistics Manager via email and opens a formal incident ticket. The
   customer is proactively notified with a revised ETA.
3. **Tier 3 (48+ hours late OR any critical shipment):** Escalate immediately
   to the Head of Logistics and the Account Manager. A recovery plan must be
   documented within 4 hours of the escalation.

## Customer Communication
For all Tier 2 and Tier 3 escalations, the Account Manager owns customer
communication. Coordinators must never commit to compensation or refunds
without Account Manager approval.

## Service Credits
Service credits for delayed critical shipments require Head of Logistics
sign-off and are capped at 10% of shipment value.
