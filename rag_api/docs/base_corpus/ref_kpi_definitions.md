# REF-021: Operational KPI Definitions

**Document Owner:** Business Intelligence
**Version:** 1.4

## Inventory Aging
**Definition:** Inventory aging measures the number of days a stock-keeping
unit (SKU) has remained in the warehouse since its receipt date, without being
sold or dispatched. It is calculated as the difference between the current date
and the goods-receipt date for each SKU.

**Why it matters:** High inventory aging ties up working capital, increases
storage cost, and raises the risk of obsolescence or spoilage. SKUs aging
beyond 90 days are flagged for clearance review.

**Target:** Average inventory aging should remain below 45 days.

## Order Fulfillment Rate
**Definition:** The percentage of customer orders shipped complete and on time
relative to total orders in a period.
**Target:** 98% or higher.

## On-Time Delivery (OTD)
**Definition:** The percentage of shipments delivered on or before the
committed delivery date.
**Target:** 95% or higher.

## Stockout Rate
**Definition:** The percentage of SKUs that reached zero available quantity
during the period while demand existed.
**Target:** Below 2%.
